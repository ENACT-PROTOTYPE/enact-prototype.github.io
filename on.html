#10

The NDA supports improvement in the quality of diabetes care by enabling participating NHS services and organisations to:

  • Assess local practice against NICE guidelines
  • Compare their care and care outcomes with similar services and organisations
  • Identify gaps or shortfalls that are priorities for improvement

  • Provide comprehensive national pictures of diabetes care and outcomes in England and Wales

Patient Story

#16

Daniel Kristeen

I had struggled to keep my diabetes under control for over a year. I was taking metformin and seriously trying to improve my diet. My Mum lost her sight because of diabetes and I was worried that this might happen to me. My doctor suggested that I take another medicine for my diabetes. Although I had misgivings about starting another medicine, I’m glad that I did because my diabetes is better controlled now and I have not experienced any bad side effects. I know that doctors are sometimes reluctant to recommend that patients with diabetes try another medicine but it is only fair to the patient that they ask.

RECOMMENDATIONS

#3
  • Identify adult patients with type 2 diabetes whose HbA1c level is 58mmol/mol or above.
  • Identify which of these patients have been treated with a single drug for 6 months or longer.
  • Invite these patients for a consultation to discuss HbA1c test results.
  • Discuss treatment options and offer dual-therapy, if appropriate.

For further information on target levels and treatment options, see NICE guidance: https://cks.nice.org.uk/diabetes-type-2#!scenario
